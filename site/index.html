Who Funds Treasury General Account Rebuilds?

When the U.S. Treasury rebuilds its cash balance, where does the money come from? We identify 43 rapid rebuild episodes from 2005–2026 and use auction-schedule surprises to trace the causal funding channels. The answer: money market funds, drawing on the Fed's ON RRP facility.

The TGA Rebuild Problem

The Treasury General Account (TGA) is the U.S. government's checking account at the Federal Reserve. When Treasury issues new securities—particularly short-term bills—the proceeds flow into the TGA, draining liquidity from the financial system. These "rebuild" episodes can move hundreds of billions of dollars in weeks, yet the transmission channels are poorly understood.

This project asks: who actually provides the funding? Drag to zoom, hover for values, double-click to reset.

Key Findings

1. Money Market Funds Are the Primary Absorbers

Using a bill-size surprise instrument (see Methods), we find that a one-standard-deviation ($19B) unexpected increase in weekly bill issuance causes MMF Treasury holdings to rise by $41B over 4 weeks (t=3.4, Newey-West).

Central result: Unexpected bill issuance that rebuilds the TGA is primarily funded by money market funds, with ~75% of the MMF absorption sourced from ON RRP runoff. Bank deposits, reserves, and bank Treasury holdings show no significant causal response.

2. Impulse Responses: Binary vs Bill-Size Surprise

The interactive charts below compare impulse response functions from two shock specifications. Blue = binary shock (contaminated by pre-trends). Red = bill-size surprise (clean identification). The vertical dotted line marks the shock date. Hover for exact values.

The bill-surprise specification eliminates spurious Bank T&A and Deposits responses while strengthening MMF Treasury and ON RRP channels.

3. Pre-Trend Scorecard

A clean shock should show no significant movement before it occurs. The binary shock fails this test in 5 of 6 channels. The bill-surprise passes.

4. Funding Channels Have Evolved Over Time

The dominant funding channel has shifted across eras:

2009–2013: reserve-driven. 2014–2019: mixed reserves & deposits. 2020–2022: diversified with ON RRP and MMF emerging.

5. The ON RRP Buffer

ON RRP declines by $31B per 1-std shock (t=−5.0). The mechanism: MMFs redeem ON RRP balances to buy bills, channeling money from the Fed's balance sheet to the TGA without touching bank deposits.

Methods

Data Pipeline

Weekly panel: 1,068 observations, 27 variables, 2005–2026. All values in millions of USD. TGA from DTS Wednesday closing balances (not FRED WTREGEN weekly average). H.8 series converted from billions. OFR monthly data forward-filled via merge_asof.

Event Detection

Flagged when weekly ΔTGA or rolling 4w/8w change exceeds the 90th percentile of positive changes only. Contiguous flagged weeks (max 9-day gap) grouped into events.

14-day staleness guard. Foreign holdings (quarterly) excluded from weekly attribution.

Auction-Schedule Surprise

surprise = offering_amt − trailing_median(last 8 same-term auctions, min 4). Summed to weekly. Tax-receipt surprise (DTS deposits) as control. Strips ~94% of predictable auction supply.

Local Projections

Δyt+h = α + β(h) · shockt + γ · controlst + εt+h

Controls: 2 shock lags, 1 response lag, 11 month dummies, tax-receipt surprise. Newey-West HAC SEs (bandwidth = horizon). Placebo tests at h=−4 to h=−1.

Caveats: (1) Identifies issuance-driven rebuilds only. (2) h=−1 ON RRP pre-trend from same-week announcement. (3) Shock persistence (autocorr=0.86) inflates h>8. (4) CMB robustness supports direction but has own pre-trends.

Limitations

  • Issuance-specific. Does not capture tax receipts, coupon issuance, or spending.
  • Weekly frequency. May miss intra-week dynamics.
  • Shock persistence. Autocorrelation = 0.86. Long-horizon effects contaminated.
  • NSA baseline. Month dummies partially control seasonality.
  • Accounting overlap. Channels are not mutually exclusive.
